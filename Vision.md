# Vision.md

## Product Name

**Modern Visual Integration Operating System**

Working name only. Final branding can change later.

---

## 1. Vision

Build the most modern and flexible platform for designing, running, and operating integrations.

This product should allow companies to visually create workflows, connect to external systems, transform and route data, monitor execution, and deploy workloads in the model that best fits their security and operational needs.

The long-term vision is to become the operating system for business integrations and data movement:
- visual enough for non-developers to understand
- powerful enough for engineers to trust
- flexible enough for enterprises to adopt
- open enough for partners and community contributors to extend

This is not just an ETL product, and not only a workflow tool. It is a unified platform that combines orchestration, connector management, workflow design, execution reliability, observability, and ecosystem extensibility.

---

## 2. Mission

Enable teams to build, run, and scale production-grade integrations faster, with far better usability and much more deployment flexibility than traditional integration platforms.

---

## 3. Problem Statement

Current integration and data movement tools often force painful trade-offs:
- strong capabilities but outdated UX
- no-code convenience but weak extensibility
- managed SaaS convenience but poor deployment flexibility
- connector breadth but limited customization
- workflow automation but weak production observability
- enterprise control but high setup and maintenance cost

Teams struggle with:
- slow connector development
- poor visibility into failures
- fragmented tooling for sync, automation, and orchestration
- difficulty supporting customer-specific deployments
- inability to combine no-code and developer-grade approaches cleanly
- limited support for a connector ecosystem and marketplace model

There is room for a new category: a modern visual integration platform with a decoupled execution model and a strong ecosystem strategy.

---

## 4. Product Thesis

The winning product is not “another ETL tool.”

The winning product is a **modern visual integration operating system** that combines:
- a premium visual workflow experience
- a decoupled control plane and execution plane
- a pluggable connector framework
- hybrid and dedicated deployment options
- a no-code plus low-code plus code operating model
- strong observability and governance
- an ecosystem that lets third parties contribute connectors and workflow components

This combination creates a stronger business and product position than competing only on connector count or raw pipeline features.

---

## 5. Core Product Pillars

### 5.1 Visual Orchestration
Users should be able to design workflows using a node-based editor that is intuitive, inspectable, and powerful.

### 5.2 Reliable Execution
Workflows must execute with production-grade reliability, retries, checkpoints, logging, and clear error handling.

### 5.3 Connector Extensibility
The platform must make it easy to build, publish, certify, and operate connectors.

### 5.4 Deployment Flexibility
Customers must be able to choose shared cloud, hybrid, dedicated, or self-hosted execution models.

### 5.5 Great User Experience
The product should feel like a modern SaaS application, not a legacy admin console.

### 5.6 Ecosystem and Network Effects
The platform should create long-term defensibility through connector and workflow ecosystem participation.

---

## 6. Target Users

### Primary Users
- Product and platform teams at B2B SaaS companies
- Data and analytics teams needing production-grade integrations
- Internal automation and integration teams
- Solution architects and technical operators

### Secondary Users
- Business users configuring standard workflows
- Partners building connectors for customers
- Agencies and service providers managing integrations for multiple clients

### Enterprise Stakeholders
- Security and compliance teams
- IT operations and cloud platform teams
- Procurement and architecture review groups

---

## 7. Ideal Customer Profiles

### ICP 1: B2B SaaS teams
Companies that want to offer integrations inside their own product without becoming an integration engineering company.

### ICP 2: Mid-market data and integration teams
Teams that want modern UX, custom connector flexibility, and better deployment choices than legacy tools offer.

### ICP 3: Enterprise buyers with stricter infrastructure requirements
Organizations that need control-plane convenience but customer-controlled execution due to governance, security, or residency requirements.

---

## 8. Value Proposition

### For Product Teams
Ship customer-facing integrations faster with a white-label-ready visual platform.

### For Engineers
Retain control through APIs, SDKs, versioning, and code escape hatches.

### For Operations Teams
Get visibility, reliability, governance, and deployment flexibility.

### For Enterprises
Adopt the platform without forcing all workloads into vendor-managed infrastructure.

### For Partners and Community Builders
Contribute and potentially monetize connectors and reusable workflow components.

---

## 9. Strategic Differentiators

### 9.1 Modern Workflow UX
A visual experience that is pleasant, understandable, and operationally useful.

### 9.2 Control Plane / Data Plane Separation
A product architecture that allows premium SaaS experience with flexible execution location.

### 9.3 Ecosystem-Friendly Design
Connectors and workflow blocks are not hard-coded product internals; they are extensible platform entities.

### 9.4 No-Code to Code Continuum
Users can start with no-code and move into low-code or code without leaving the platform.

### 9.5 Embeddable Product Surface
The product can power internal operations and customer-facing embedded integration experiences.

---

## 10. Feature System Overview

The product should support the following major capability areas:
- Visual workflow studio
- Workflow execution engine
- Connector framework and SDK
- Connector marketplace
- No-code connector builder
- Data transformation layer
- Scheduling and triggers
- Observability and debugging
- Secrets and credential management
- Multi-tenant workspace model
- Versioning and lifecycle management
- Embedded integration toolkit
- API-first platform operations
- Template library
- Governance and compliance controls
- Billing and usage management
- Scalable execution infrastructure

These capability areas will be prioritized in phases, but the architecture should assume them from the start.

---

## 11. Business Model Direction

The business should support multiple monetization layers:
- SaaS subscription tiers
- usage-based or capacity-based pricing
- enterprise deployment premiums
- marketplace revenue share
- professional services and implementation support

The product should be designed so monetization can evolve without reworking the technical core.

---

## 12. Go-to-Market Direction

### Recommended Initial Wedge
Start with companies that need modern, embedded, or operational integration workflows and care about product experience.

### Why This Wedge
- pain is immediate and visible
- product UX matters more
- deployment flexibility is commercially valuable
- white-label integration features can command premium pricing

### Expansion Path
1. Start with core orchestration and connectors
2. Expand to marketplace and community ecosystem
3. Add stronger workflow templates and industry-specific packs
4. Introduce more advanced AI-assisted authoring later

---

## 13. Product Principles

### Principle 1: Reliability First
Pretty UX is important, but trust in execution is non-negotiable.

### Principle 2: Visual by Default, Programmable by Design
The platform should be approachable without becoming limiting.

### Principle 3: Platform, Not Just Product
Features should be built in a way that supports future ecosystem growth.

### Principle 4: Enterprise-Ready Without Enterprise-Only Complexity
The product should scale up to enterprise needs without feeling heavy for smaller teams.

### Principle 5: Clear Boundaries Between Authoring and Execution
The visual builder should not dictate the runtime architecture.

### Principle 6: UX Is a Strategic Asset
Usability is not just presentation. It is a product advantage.

---

## 14. Success Outcomes

If the product succeeds, customers should experience:
- faster time to build and deploy integrations
- lower operational burden for syncs and workflows
- better visibility into failures and runtime health
- easier creation of custom connectors
- more confidence adopting the platform in regulated or enterprise environments
- stronger collaboration between business users and engineers

At the business level, success means:
- recurring SaaS revenue
- premium enterprise deals through dedicated execution options
- ecosystem-led expansion through connectors and templates
- durable differentiation through architecture and product experience

---

## 15. Non-Goals for Early Stages

The first versions of the product should not try to solve every adjacent category at once.

Avoid early dilution into:
- full customer data platform scope
- broad reverse ETL platform ambitions
- advanced AI-first product surface before workflow fundamentals are stable
- connector quantity arms race without quality and maintainability
- building every possible vertical package before the platform core is proven

---

## 16. Long-Term Ambition

Over time, this platform should become the standard environment where integration logic is:
- designed visually
- deployed flexibly
- operated safely
- extended openly
- consumed by teams and products alike

The final aspiration is to be the default integration operating layer for modern software organizations.
